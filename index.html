Tactical Outlook: what will shape BESS term-sheet base cases in Gujarat (2025–26)

Regulators have anchored price discovery and operating-cost assumptions around what the state has authorized in 2025. Below is a practical modelling scaffold—capacity rent benchmarks, banking/open-access drag, tariff compression signals, and developer-scope capex that now sits squarely in your base case.

Capacity rent (approved) ₹2.80–₹2.856 lakh/MW-month
Banking charge (OA) ₹1.50/kWh (through Mar 31, 2026)
Phase VII discovery ≈₹1.85–₹1.89 lakh/MW-month
Scope risk Interconnection + metering + bay works
Modeling intent: If you bake (A) discovered tariff ceilings, (B) a sticky banking cost floor, and (C) full interconnection & metering capex into term sheets now, you reduce renegotiation risk once BESPA discussions harden.

What changed (and why it matters)

Four items that most directly move cashflows, covenants, and contingency buffers

  1. BESS capacity rental benchmarks reset after competitive discovery
    GERC-approved discovered tariffs for a 500 MW / 1,000 MWh tranche sit around ₹280,000–₹285,600 per MW-month—use as a 2025–26 benchmark for stable capacity rent.
  2. Banking / open-access charges remain a real drag
    The green energy banking charge is retained at ₹1.50/kWh through March 31, 2026—stress test at this level and a higher scenario.
  3. Phase VII and beyond signal tariff compression + tighter obligations
    Phase VII (2,000 MW / 4,000 MWh) discovered ≈₹1.85–₹1.89 lakh/MW-month—implying tighter margins and higher sensitivity to availability/performance.
  4. Technical scope is explicitly developer risk
    Interconnection to GETCO plus associated connectivity, bay works and metering are in developer scope—budget upfront and protect with contingencies and timelines.

Immediate term-sheet guardrails

  • Base case: lock capacity rent assumptions to discovered/approved benchmarks; keep conservative escalation/Indexation stance.
  • Opex drag: treat ₹1.50/kWh as a floor; include adverse case for higher charges or tighter banking terms.
  • Capex scope: include line + substation bay + metering + commissioning interface costs and schedule float.
  • Performance risk: model penalties / availability deductions explicitly; tighter tariffs amplify downside.
